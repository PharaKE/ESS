Opinium Global Entrepreneurship Index 2025

A strategic analysis of 137 global markets benchmarked across 15 years. Key findings on what drives and hinders entrepreneurship worldwide.

2025 Edition 137 Markets 26 Indicators 15 Year Track 5 Pillars
Key Findings
Six headline conclusions from the 2025 report
01
West Retains Overall Lead — For Now
North America and Europe maintain top positions with North America scoring 77/100. However, East Asia & Pacific is closing rapidly, now at 60/100 vs Europe's 66/100.
02
Asian Tigers Dominate Elite Rankings
Singapore (#1, 96/100) and Hong Kong (#2, 94/100) hold the top. Macao ranks 4th. The Nordics Denmark (#3), Sweden (#6) lead Europe. All Nordic & Benelux nations rank in the global Top 20.
03
UK Stuck Outside Top 20 for 6 Years
The UK ranks 23rd at 79/100, down 3 pts from 2011. Connectedness dropped 9 pts due to lagging digital infrastructure. Strong governance (88/100) isn't enough to break back in.
04
EU Losing Edge to APAC Competition
The EU's average dropped 3 pts to 74/100. Economic & Financial Access fell 8 pts, linked to post-2008 austerity and tighter banking regulations.
05
USA Falls to Rank 30 Despite Strength
The US scores 75/100, ranking 30th. Financial strength (86/100) is offset by Human Capital challenges (-8 pts since 2011) and trade barriers.
06
Gender Gap Remains Critical
Women received just 1.8% of equity investment in H1 2024. The Invest in Women Taskforce has committed over £250m to address this structural gap.
Strategic Takeaways
Actionable insights for policymakers, investors and entrepreneurs
Infrastructure Investment is Non-Negotiable
Countries falling in Connectedness UK (-9 pts) and EU (-7 pts) show digital infrastructure is no longer optional. Full-fibre broadband, 5G, and electricity access directly correlate with startup density.
Access to Finance Remains the #1 Structural Barrier
The EU's 8-point decline in Economic & Financial Access reflects a global truth: post crisis austerity disproportionately hurts entrepreneurs who rely on credit, equity, and government-backed lending.
Asia-Pacific is the World's Rising Entrepreneurship Powerhouse
Singapore and Hong Kong's sustained leadership reflects deliberate policy choices. The UAE (now #11, up 12 places since 2011) shows rapid reform can shift rankings dramatically within a decade.
Cambodia's Path: From 23 to 37 — Growth Trajectory but Governance Gap
Cambodia improved 14 points since 2011 one of the best growth stories in Southeast Asia. Economic & Financial Access (51/100) is strong. However, a governance score of 17/100 is the single largest constraint.
Geopolitical Instability Destroys Entrepreneurial Momentum Rapidly
Russia's 18-place drop (to rank 79) is the most dramatic example. Hungary (-11) and Poland (-7) also show how policy uncertainty translates almost immediately into weaker entrepreneurial environments.

🇰🇭 Strategic Analysis for Khmer Enterprise
Strengths & Opportunities
• Strong Growth Momentum: 14 point gain since 2011 one of SEA's best improvement stories.

• Financial Access Surprise: Score of 51/100 in Economic & Financial Access outperforms the overall index, suggesting the financial ecosystem is developing faster than governance.

• Digital Economy Entry: Connectedness at 43/100 shows rapid mobile adoption fertile ground for digital entrepreneurs and fintech innovators.

• Young Demographics: Cambodia's young population creates a growing consumer market and expanding labour pool for Human Capital improvement.
Challenges & Priority Areas
• Governance Gap (17/100): Cambodia's most critical constraint. Rule of law, corruption control, and regulatory transparency must improve to advance beyond rank 100.

• Market Potential (38/100): Limited trade intensity and domestic market size constrain entrepreneur scale up. Regional economic integration is key.

• Skills & Human Capital (37/100): Investment in digital skills, STEM education, and vocational training is essential for building the talent pipeline.

• KE Priority: Programs addressing governance literacy, cross border trade facilitation, and digital skills align with Cambodia's highest leverage improvement areas.
